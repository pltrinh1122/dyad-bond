# DIP refinement — the `craft-*` family + assertion-timing

> **A proposal to the form's DIP** (`AGENT.md`). It renames Dimension #5 (**NON-NEGOTIABLE**) and
> Dimension #1's *tended target* into one **`craft-*` family**, and specifies **when each is asserted**
> during instantiation. Structure only — craft-agnostic, every dyad's fills differ. bond's own fills are
> the worked exemplar, not the proposal.

## 1 · The problems with the status quo

1. **"NON-NEGOTIABLE" names the guard, not the thing guarded.** It foregrounds the defensive modality —
   *can't-compromise / fails-first* — not the dyad's **constitutive value**. The term is borrowed and
   negative where the intent is positive and constitutive.
2. **It conflates two levels.** A Healer's *"verify-before-asserting"* reads as a **rule**; dyad-bond's
   *"be covalent"* reads as a **value**. Same slot, two registers — because the slot silently bundles *the
   cherished value* and *the rule that protects it*.
3. **The DIP under-specifies assertion-timing.** It says Dimension #5 is craft-dependent and discovered,
   but not the load-bearing fact: **a new Operator cannot assert it at birth.** You can't name what
   fails-first until you've been under pressure, nor forge a rule from a breach you haven't suffered.

## 2 · The `craft-*` family — three levels

| level | the question | who fills it, and when | bond's fill *(exemplar)* |
|---|---|---|---|
| **`craft-telos`** | *why* — the tended purpose | **Operator asserts at instantiation** | tend the interior craft of the dyadic relationship |
| **`craft-value`** | *what is cherished* — the elected boundary-value | **placeholder** — Operator elects, agent surfaces; materializes through practice | covalence |
| **`craft-invariant`** | *what must hold* — the rule(s) protecting the value | **placeholder** — agent proposes (forged from breach), Operator disposes | `keep covalent` + its enablers |

The three are a layered family — *why → what-is-cherished → what-must-hold* — not synonyms. `craft-telos`
renames Dimension #1's *tended target*; `craft-value` + `craft-invariant` replace Dimension #5's NON-NEGOTIABLE.

## 3 · The assertion-timing principle *(the core of the proposal)*

**At instantiation, only `craft-telos` is asserted.** The Operator brings their purpose — it is *why they
came*, and everything else serves it. **`craft-value` and `craft-invariant` are placeholders that
materialize through practice**; they cannot be asserted at birth.

The fill-mechanisms differ — the covalent division of labor, applied per level:

- **`craft-telos`** — **Operator asserts.** Terminal intent is human-sourced; only the human originates it.
- **`craft-value`** — **Operator elects, agent surfaces** candidates. The dyad discovers what it cherishes by
  living, then the Operator elects it.
- **`craft-invariant`** — **agent proposes** (from a breach), **Operator disposes.** The detector/disposer split.

This is not a new mechanism: it **generalizes the DIP's own 8th dimension** — *tenet-alive "materializes,
not asserted."* `craft-value`/`craft-invariant` materialize the same way.

## 4 · Grounding

- **The terminal/operational split** (bond's `relationship-craft.md §1`): terminal intent is human-sourced
  → `craft-telos` is the Operator's to assert; operational patterns are agent-available-and-dutied →
  `craft-value`/`craft-invariant` are agent-surfaced/proposed. The timing *falls out of* this split.
- **bond's own history is the proof.** Day one had only the telos. The **covalent-value** was elected across
  many sessions; **`bond:C1`** was *forged* from the `dyad-loom` breach — you cannot assert *"don't
  rubber-stamp"* until you have rubber-stamped. The DIP, today, would have asked bond to assert at birth a
  guard it had not yet earned.
- **The DIP's existing generativity** — each dyad discovers its own discipline through SPAOR — is exactly
  this; the proposal only *names the timing the DIP already implies* and corrects the term.

## 5 · Universality *(the Founding-gate bar)*

Craft-agnostic — every dyad has all three, only the fills vary:

| dyad | `craft-telos` | `craft-value` | `craft-invariant` |
|---|---|---|---|
| dyad-bond | the relationship craft | covalence | keep covalent + enablers |
| dyad-healer | heal / rescue | *(elected)* | verify-before-asserting |
| *a fresh dyad* | *Operator asserts* | *placeholder* | *placeholder* |

## 6 · What changes in `AGENT.md`

- **Dimension #1:** *tended target* → **`craft-telos`** — asserted by the Operator at instantiation.
- **Dimension #5:** *NON-NEGOTIABLE* → **`craft-value`** (placeholder, Operator-elected) + **`craft-invariant`**
  (placeholder, agent-proposed) — seeded *empty* at instantiation, materialized through the bootstrap's cycles.
- **Bootstrap flow:** assert `craft-telos`; carry `craft-value`/`craft-invariant` as agent-proposed
  placeholders that fill as SPAOR cycles surface them — like the 8th dimension already does.
