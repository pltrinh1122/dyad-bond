# Evidentiary Discipline — artifact-under-cost, not mutual self-report

*Settled knowledge, kb-with-caveat. Authored `dialectic/relationship-craft.md §Cycle 1` (F4), verdict
landed 2026-05-31 — this file holds the graduated rule; the dialectic entry collapses to a pointer.*

## The rule

**Evidence = artifact-under-cost; mutual warmth ≠ corroboration.** A felt sense of agreement — even
when *both* halves of a dyad report it — does not itself strengthen a claim. "We both agree this felt
good" is one felt-report, counted once; it pays no falsification cost and so adds no evidentiary
weight, however mutual. What counts as evidence is a produced **artifact** that survived a genuine
attempt to kill it — the cost paid is what makes it evidence, not the agreement surrounding it.

Corollary (self-report is testimony, not telemetry): a party's own account of their state or
reasoning ("I was distracted," "I felt X") is commentary, not an independently-verified data point.
It may explain a finding after the fact; it cannot substitute for one.

## Falsification status — survives, not settled

**Origin (F4, `relationship-craft.md §Cycle 1`, RUN 2026-05-31 → SURVIVED-WITH-AMENDMENT):** the
dyad turned its own falsification-dividend mechanism on its own founding evidence — "both halves
agreed it felt good" (Tenet-alive #8), precisely the peak-grain moment the rule itself would
distrust. Verdict: the mutual agreement was **demoted** from corroboration to mere felt-report (no
extra cost paid); the mechanism flagged its own founding record as suspect and held — self-
application, not exemption. **Residual limit (honest, un-smoothed):** artifacts produced under the
mechanism (a role-name, a NON-NEGOTIABLE, a Telos) prove the mechanism *ran* — cost paid, synthesis
produced — but do not themselves prove any *felt* dividend was real; the felt layer stays
self-report on both sides of the ledger.

**Coverage gap, named not hidden:** one closed test, intra-dyad. No cross-dyad or cross-human attack
has run against this rule. Per `theory-pipeline.yaml`'s `graduation_rule`, caps at `survives`, not
