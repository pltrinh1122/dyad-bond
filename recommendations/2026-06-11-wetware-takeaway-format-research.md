## Headline (the evidence's own one-liner)

The end-of-session take-away should be **a short set of self-generated, OPEN retrieval prompts centered
on the session's unresolved HARD problems — NOT a re-readable closed summary.** Question-form over
statement-form; open over closed; effortful-but-answerable over answer-giving; loaded before rest.

## Confirmed findings (survived 3-vote adversarial verification)

1. **Active retrieval beats re-reading (~g=0.50) — build it from QUESTIONS, not a summary.** Two
   independent meta-analyses converge (Rowland 2014 g=0.50, 81% favor testing; Adesope 2017 g≈0.51,
   272 effects). Dunlosky 2013: practice-testing + distributed-practice = HIGH utility; **re-reading +
   summarization = LOW utility.** Retrieval *itself* (no feedback/restudy) is the active ingredient.
   `3-0`.
2. **For DELAYED/offline synthesis specifically, repeated retrieval wins the crossover.** Roediger &
   Karpicke: study-once-test-thrice gave the best **1-week** recall (~56% vs ~42%), even though repeated
   study won the *immediate* test. The Operator's goal is delayed synthesis → favors prompts over a
   re-readable summary. `2-1`.
3. **Self-GENERATED content remembered ~0.40 SD better than received.** Bertsch 2007 (0.40 SD, 86
   studies); McCurdy 2020 (126 articles). **The use-case tension, named:** the brief says *the agent
   writes the take-away*, but generation says *the human* should. **Resolution: the agent writes PROMPTS
   that force the Operator to generate/retrieve — never hands over finished conclusions.** `3-0`.
4. **Prompt form: OPEN/under-constrained + effortful (desirable difficulty).** McCurdy: generation
   *constraint* significantly moderates the benefit. Karpicke: more cues → smaller effect. **But**
   (the 2-1 caveat / Kornell): difficulty that tips into outright failure *with no feedback* is
   counterproductive → **effortful-but-answerable, not impossible.** `3-0` constraint / `2-1` effort.
5. **Leave the core problems OPEN/UNRESOLVED — incubation reliably helps, most for DIVERGENT problems.**
   Sio & Ormerod 2009 (~117 studies, mean d=0.29; divergent > insight tasks). Closed conclusions give
   nothing to incubate. **Effect is modest (low-medium) → real, not dramatic.** `3-0`.
6. **Incubation is PROBLEM-SPECIFIC — name the concrete carried-forward problem, not "keep thinking."**
   McDaniel 2025 (preregistered): mind-wandering improved creativity only for the **same** prompt after
   the break, not a new one. The take-away must name the *specific* unresolved problem so offline
   mind-wandering reattaches. `3-0` (correlational, single study).
7. **Load HARD, PARTIALLY-solved problems before rest; prefer SLEEP.** Sio/Monaghan/Ormerod 2013: sleep
   helped *difficult* remote-associates (.293 vs .147 wake), **none** for easy; a 12h *wake* gap gave no
   benefit (sleep, not mere time, drives it). Wagner 2004: sleep ~**tripled** insight (59% vs ~20%) —
   **but only for those with pre-existing implicit/partial knowledge.** → surface hard problems the
   Operator *already has partial traction on*, right before rest. `3-0` / `2-1` on the partial-knowledge
   precondition (single paradigm).
8. **Fill the offline interval with UNDEMANDING low-load activity (or sleep) — not idleness, not heavy
   load.** Sio & Ormerod: low-demand tasks > rest (for linguistic); Gilhooly 2016: undemanding
   interpolated task > demanding > none. The take-away can recommend the *re-engagement context* (a walk,
   chores), not just content. `3-0` / `2-1` rest-nuance.

## ⚠ REFUTED (killed by vote — do NOT design these in)

- **Recall ≫ recognition format** — `1-2`, did NOT survive. **Do not assume multiple-choice is inferior**
  to open recall for the priming artifact; the format-superiority claim is not robust here.
- **Pair each question with an answer key / feedback** — `0-3`, **decisively killed.** Do NOT append
  answers to the prompts; the "feedback markedly helps" claim did not survive (and it would also kill the
  desirable-difficulty + open-incubation goal). The prompts stay *unanswered*.
- **Generation effect is larger at long delay** — `1-2`. Don't lean on a *growing*-with-delay story; the
  generation benefit is real but not established as delay-amplified.

## Caveats (the evidence's own honesty)

- **Format specifics (word count, item count, exact open:closed ratio) are NOT directly evidenced** —
  no study compared 3 vs 10 items. "Few, effortful, problem-specific" follows from desirable-difficulty +
  cognitive-load *logic*, not a head-to-head trial. Treat the numbers in any proposal as reasoned
  extrapolation, falsifiable by use.
- **Offline-synthesis effect sizes are MODEST** (d≈0.29 incubation, g≈0.22 immediate) — reliable, small.
- **Sleep-insight precondition** (needs pre-existing partial knowledge) rests largely on one paradigm
  (number-reduction) → generality to arbitrary work problems is an extrapolation.
- **No literature on AI-agent/human recurring-session take-aways exists** — all grounding is by analogy
  from education / creative problem-solving / memory research. (= an UNOCCUPIED niche; cf. the s13
  calibration frontier-gift — a possible AGENT.md frontier-track candidate.)

## Open questions the research could NOT close (→ feed the proposal's own open items)

1. **Optimal NUMBER of open items** before overload/dilution — untested (Zeigarnik multi-item intrusion
   vs single-focal unresolved).
2. **Right OPEN:CLOSED ratio** — incubation needs *open*; sleep-insight needs *partial/closed
   scaffolding*. How much settled ground must ride along with each open question?
3. **Best prompt TEMPLATE** to force self-generation given recall-vs-recognition + feedback were refuted
   (cloze? self-explanation? elaborative-interrogation "why"?). Matuschak/Anki principles named but not
   independently grounded in surviving claims.
4. **Implementation intentions (Gollwitzer)** as re-engagement cues ("when X, I'll reconsider Y") — named
   in the brief, **no surviving claim addressed it** → untested here, candidate to probe.
