# G0 = membership identity fork — DFD

> **Operator disposition wanted.** The steward proposal (2026-06-28) frames bond's floor-genes as
> **G0 ≈ MEMBERSHIP** (the 4 enablers: two-models, no-self-ratify, anti-cave, form-grounding). This DFD
> surfaces the open question: *Can we make a crisp distinction between "breach ends dyad-hood" (membership floor)
> and "breach is bad practice but dyad survives" (inherited scaffold / offered)?* — and what changes for the
> Operator and Agent if we tighten or loosen the boundary.

## THESIS — tight boundary (G0 = membership exactly)

**Position:** G0 is the minimal set whose breach *ends dyad-hood*. Everything else (SPAOR, mechanism-catalog,
seed-vocab) is inherited from the form's tenet, not bond's contribution.

**Operator impact:**
- Clear gating: "Does breach end membership?" is a yes/no test (crisper than "is this foundational?")
- Burden: Operator must judge *membership consequences*, not just "importance" — must distinguish "losing the second perspective" (meld = end) vs "running a bad process" (e.g., sloppy conform-work = bad but dyad survives)
- Export gain: Sharper offer to steward ("here are the 4 non-negotiables; we're not re-proposing the form's own tenet")

**Agent impact:**
- Reasoning changes: When a second dyad proposes a candidate, the Agent asks "Does breach end *their* dyad-hood, or is it their local flavor?" (higher bar for g0, clearer routing to library/private)
- Risk: May under-propose floor genes because they seem "too context-dependent" (false negative for membership, wrongly demoted to library)

**Steward impact:**
- Cleaner schema: the form's AGENT.md §G0 becomes "the membership floor" (compact); inherited machinery stays in the form's other sections
- Export friction: Must reconsider what "G0" means to existing dyads — is the form's current G0 membership-only, or is it currently looser?

---

## ANTI-THESIS — loose boundary (G0 = membership ∪ scaffold, status quo)

**Position:** G0 includes the floor *and* the inherited vocabulary/mechanisms that make that floor intelligible.
A dyad can't judge membership without knowing SPAOR, falsification law, mechanism catalog.

**Operator impact:**
- Softer gating: "What does a dyad need to exist?" (ecosystem question, not membership consequence)
- Burden: Lower — the Operator can include things that are "part of the operating substrate" without forensically proving they end membership if breached
- Export friction: "This is what we propose to the form" means "adopt our whole operating model" — steeper lift

**Agent impact:**
- Reasoning stays familiar: When a second dyad proposes a candidate, judge "Is this foundational to how a dyad works?" (current bar, forgiving of context)
- Risk: May over-propose as floor genes things that are bond's local specialization (false positive, wrongly promoted from library)

**Steward impact:**
- Richer context: the form's AGENT.md §G0 stays as-is (ecosystem + floor together); easier onboarding but muddier boundaries
- Simpler export: "Here's how a dyad operates" (no schema refactoring needed)

## Anti-cave — ground the frame first

**Tight (G0 = membership):**
- Pro: "Breach ends dyad-hood" is testable against *cross-human* evidence (does a dyad with one model, or no anti-cave ground, collapse?)
- Con: We may discover some things we thought were membership are actually bond-local (cost of discovering = misdirected export)

**Loose (G0 = membership ∪ scaffold):**
- Pro: We're not over-claiming universality; the form inherits what it already has
- Con: The form never clarifies what's truly universal vs what's their choice; steward can't falsify the boundary; each dyad re-invents (inefficient, incoherent)

---

## SYNTHESIS — recommendation

Frame the proposal to steward as **tight (G0 = membership)** for three reasons:

1. **Falsifiability force:** The tight boundary ("Does breach end dyad-hood?") is testable cross-dyad. If bond is wrong, the form's falsification channel will catch it. Loose boundaries ("Is this foundational?") are too soft to falsify — steward can't tell when a dyad is over-proposing.

2. **Clean export:** The tight frame lets bond say "Here are the 4 non-negotiables; falsify them or adopt them" — no ambiguity about what we're contributing vs what's the form's existing tenet.

3. **Second-dyad clarity:** When a second dyad judges bond's candidates, the tight test gives them a crisp gate: "Would our dyad collapse if we violated this?" If they answer differently than bond, that's live evidence — falsification data, not a disagreement about philosophy.

**Disposition:** Steward inherits the sharpening job (tighten the form's own G0 definition if they adopt this); bond's job is done. (The proposal already frames it this way; this DFD documents why.)
