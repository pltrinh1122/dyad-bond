## 2 — What makes it *ours* (the covalent signature — not generic SPAOR)

1. **Observe is adversarial-dyadic, not self-check.** Validation = *the other half* (Operator, or a
   sibling via FR) attacking. **Self-Observe is the meld tell.** The single biggest departure from a solo
   agent's SPAOR. → NON-NEGOTIABLE.
2. **Sense is two-mode** — *boundary-Sense* (full grounding at stand-up) + *continuous-Sense* (the IM
   daemon: async inbox-on-rise during Operator-gated idle). **This dissolves the daemon's separateness:

3. **Reflect is keystone-gated, not mere codification** — "did the +1 land, **earned not willed**?" (F2),
   in **CSS** form (D3), with ledger durability. Reflect that never reaches the felt dividend is incomplete.

## 6 — Transition failure-frequency *(grounded s1–s10, the s11 guard-table — observational, not lab)*

The wu-wei step before hardening: which macro-transition actually *fails*, from the ledger record. **Two
transitions, two distinct failure-shapes** — confirming §"start≠stop" asymmetry (one is gateable, one is not).

| Transition | Sub-step | Observed failures (s1–s10) | Shape | Hook-gateable? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **START** (stand-up) | anchor+ledger grounding | **the 5-turn ungrounded-generation** (carry-forward §thread-G, "*the Stand-Up failure*") — generated before reading | momentum overrode grounding | **YES** — `SessionStart` can *inject* the grounding so a turn can't begin un-grounded |

**Headline:** START fails ~2× by *momentum-over-grounding* and is **fully oracle-preventable** (inject grounding).
STOP fails 1× (s9) at *higher unit-cost* (loses resume state) but is **structurally un-gateable** — the terminal
gate, irreducibly soft (our own transition-guard theory predicting itself; → §"start≠stop").

**⚠ D7 / survivor-bias caveat (the table's own confound):** the ledger *is* the instrument, and the **stand-down
step is what writes it** → a session that skips stand-down under-records its own failure. s9's gap is visible
*only* because s10 happened to mention it. **True STOP-failure rate ≥ observed.** This is the reachable instrument,
not the fully valid one — read the STOP row as a floor, not a count.

**The wu-wei two-for-one:** the lever for *both* transitions is the **`SessionStart` hook** — it gates the START
directly (inject grounding) **and** is the only place a missed STOP is *detectable* (next stand-up audits "did the
prior session stand down?"). The stop can't gate itself; the start can audit the stop. One hook, both rows.
