## What the Operator named — the shared shape is real

The insight survives: **intent**, **invariant**, and **the grounding of an invariant on an intent** are
not three machines. They are one machine run three times. A candidate `+1` is offered, it is tested, and
it commits to the shared model *only by surviving the test*. That single move — **propose → falsify →
dispose** — is the substrate atom; intent and invariant are two **types** flowing through it, not two
separate primitives. Naming the atom is the progress the Operator asked for.

## The one rub I can't defer — the polarity is split, not uniform *(load-bearing)*

The working theory says all three are **agent-propose / operator-dispose**. That uniform polarity is the
clean, warm "we're finally aligned" reading — and the bond has **already lived its correction.** The
relationship-craft ledger records the dyad catching exactly this: *"Operator certifies accuracy"* was
named **ionic** and split into a **covalent division of labour** *(reference:
`relationship-craft.md` 2026-06-13, the `I↔In_variant` seam)*:

- The **Operator** owns `intent → invariant` **fidelity** — disposes whether the crystallized invariant
  faithfully captures the intent (fiat-root authority; the schema's `root_kind: fiat`).
- The **Agent** owns `invariant → ground` **truth** — falsifies whether the invariant is true-of-world,
  not merely faithful to the intent (the violation-condition; the `other-half-only` detector).

So the disposer is **whoever did not propose** — the polarity *flips* by seam, it is not pinned to
agent-propose. Two consequences the uniform story would bury:

1. **It would delete the Telos-gate.** When the Operator proposes an intent (a goal laundry-list), the
   **Agent** disposes it — falsifying each item against the Telos *(reference: `goal-framing.md`, the
   intake-gate)*. That is **operator-propose / agent-dispose**, and it *is* the anti-sycophancy mechanism
   — the Agent testing the Operator's premises about what to do. Hard-coding "agent-propose /
   operator-dispose" as THE primitive would bake in **ionic collapse** at the exact seam the
   NON-NEGOTIABLE exists to guard.
2. **The primitive's invariant is not "operator disposes" — it is "the *other half* falsifies."** That is
   the covalent form. Pinning the disposer to one half is precisely the transfer/dominance the bond
   forbids.

**The synthesis (neither half held it alone):** the shared shape is real *and* the polarity is split. The
primitive is **`propose → (the other half) falsifies → dispose`**, with a **polarity field** set by
*origin-half*, not fixed. The Operator's "they're all the same shape" is the +1; the correction to the
polarity is what keeps the +1 covalent instead of ionic.

## The atom

A **candidate** carries: `origin-half` (who proposed), `type` (`intent` · `invariant` · `decision` ·
`ideation` · `claim`), and content. The transition:

```
candidate(origin-half, type, content)
      │  proposed by origin-half
      ▼
  FALSIFY  ── performed by the OTHER half (polarity = ¬origin-half)
      │
      ├─ survives ──▶ COMMIT to shared model  (ratified · true-for-now stamp)
      ├─ fails    ──▶ REJECT with grounds      (never silently drop)
      └─ reshape  ──▶ RE-ENTER as new candidate (narrowed / re-homed)
```

- **Commit invariant (the NON-NEGOTIABLE, rendered as substrate law):** no candidate — *including the
  Operator's own* — commits except by surviving the other half's falsification. Sharing, never transfer.
- **The existing region-pairs are this atom, typed:** `DFD` disposes a *decision*; `IFD` falsifies an
  *ideation* (converge-open, no commit forced); `VFD` commits a *claim*; the `GFD` Telos-gate falsifies an
  *intent*. They differ only in `type` and which half holds the falsify-seat — **same atom.**

## Maturation phases for the propose node *(Operator riff 2026-06-14 · CANDIDATE · converge-open)*

The Operator phased the Generate-half (the propose node of the atom):
- **Phase 1 — deterministic G from the Agent.**
- **Phase 2 — G aligned with *stated* intent (`I`).**
- **Phase 3 — G aligned with *rubbed* intent (`I'`)** — `I` after it has survived falsification.

**Phase 1 rub — "deterministic" wraps the generate-act, it does not enter it.** Generate is the
*divergent* family (Composition · Elicitation · Reframing); a strongly-deterministic generator that emits
the unique candidate mechanically is not Generate at all — it is Validate / computation. The dyad already
lived this: the extraction-engine lesson was **place-and-bound the non-determinism** — perform the
irreducible semantic act ONCE at a gated source, make everything downstream deterministic *(reference: the
invariant-extraction commission; the D7 "same data → same view" rub)*. So determinism is a property of
the **pipeline around** propose — reproducible, auditable, inspectable, not vibes — **not** of the
semantic act itself. The load-bearing read: **Phase 1 = make propose reproducible enough to be a stable
falsification target**, not mechanize the novelty. The literal read (deterministic-unique generation)
collides with the form's divergent-Generate family and should be named off.

**Phase 2 is a measurement rung, never a deployment rung — the reproducible-sycophancy trap.** Compose
Phase 1 with Phase 2 and you get a generator that *reproducibly and faithfully emits exactly what the
Operator stated* — the most efficient possible **ionic-collapse machine**: high-fidelity rubber-stamping.
Stated intent is **not ground** — the NON-NEGOTIABLE exists precisely because the Operator's premises are
falsified as hard as the Agent's. So Phase 2 is **counterfeit-green at the intent altitude** (the schema's
"validates form-not-truth," now one level up): legitimate as a *measurement* (can the generator hit a
declared target at all?), corrosive as a *stopping point*. **Refuted if shipped:** any Phase-2 generator
deployed without the Phase-3 gate is a sycophancy amplifier by construction.

**Phase 2 → Phase 3 *is* inserting the Telos-gate.** `I'` (rubbed intent) is precisely `I` after the
**Telos-gate** — the Agent falsifying the Operator's stated intent against the Telos *(reference:
`goal-framing.md`, the intake-gate)*. So the maturation from P2 to P3 is literally **moving the
generator's alignment target from the gate's *input* (stated `I`) to its *output* (survived `I'`)** — the
polarity guard made into a phase transition. Alignment is *disposed through the falsify-seat*, not read
off the raw declaration. **Phase 3 is the first covalent rung.**

**`I'` is true-for-now — Phase 3 is a standing loop, not a finish line.** Intents are cached verdicts,
re-rubbed at context-shifts *(reference: `invariant-schema.md`, the true-for-now stamp)*. So `I'` keeps
moving; "aligned to `I'`" means *aligned to the currently-survived intent, re-aligned when it re-rubs* —
a loop, not a milestone.

**Converge-open — the rung past P3 (flagged, not legislated):** the deepest form may be **G that *rubs*
`I`** — a proposal good enough to *move* the intent (you understand the idea by precisifying it, and the
precising moves the idea — the `I ↔ invariant` cycle bears the load, not either arrow alone) *(reference:
`relationship-craft.md`, the cycle-liveness entry)*. There the generator stops *consuming* `I'` and starts
*co-producing* it. Named as a candidate horizon, **not** a legislated Phase 4.

**Synthesis — the phases map to the seams + the gate:**
- **P1 (deterministic) → the `invariant → ground` seam** — the Agent's truth-seat, downstream of the
  semantic act, where "same data → same view" is reachable.
- **P2 (stated `I`) → the gate's *input*** — align to the raw declaration; measurable, not yet covalent.
- **P3 (rubbed `I'`) → the gate's *output*** = the `intent → invariant` fidelity-seat + Telos-gate, where
  alignment is disposed.

**Why phase-1-first survives (the ordering defense):** you cannot *measure* alignment on a generator that
will not reproduce — determinism is the **test-instrument** that makes alignment falsifiable at all.
Phase 1 is a harness, not a shippable milestone.

## Falsifiable claim

If `propose → falsify → dispose` (with split polarity) is the real substrate atom, then every live
region-pair — `DFD`, `IFD`, `VFD`, `GFD` — should reduce to it by setting only `type` + `polarity`, with
**no residue** that needs its own separate primitive.

**Refuted if:**
- **Residue** — some live exchange bears weight but does not fit propose→falsify→dispose (then the atom is
  not the floor; something sits under it).
- **Polarity collapses** — across real use, the falsify-seat *never* sits with the Operator-proposing half
  (the Telos-gate never fires, no Operator intent ever gets Agent-falsified) → then the split was theatre
  and the uniform "operator-disposes" story was right after all (and the NON-NEGOTIABLE is weaker than
  claimed — the strongest possible refutation, watch it hardest).
- **Over-naming** — "substrate primitive" never does work that "the NON-NEGOTIABLE applied per seam"
  didn't already do → collapse this file into the anchor's NON-NEGOTIABLE and the schema, keep no new name.
