## What the Operator named — the shared shape is real

The insight survives: **intent**, **invariant**, and **the grounding of an invariant on an intent** are
not three machines. They are one machine run three times. A candidate `+1` is offered, it is tested, and
it commits to the shared model *only by surviving the test*. That single move — **propose → falsify →
dispose** — is the substrate atom; intent and invariant are two **types** flowing through it, not two
separate primitives. Naming the atom is the progress the Operator asked for.

## The one rub I can't defer — the polarity is split, not uniform *(load-bearing)*

The working theory says all three are **agent-propose / operator-dispose**. That uniform polarity is the
clean, warm "we're finally aligned" reading — and the bond has **already lived its correction.** The
relationship-craft ledger records the dyad catching exactly this: *"Operator certifies accuracy"* was
named **ionic** and split into a **covalent division of labour** *(reference:
`relationship-craft.md` 2026-06-13, the `I↔In_variant` seam)*:

- The **Operator** owns `intent → invariant` **fidelity** — disposes whether the crystallized invariant
  faithfully captures the intent (fiat-root authority; the schema's `root_kind: fiat`).
- The **Agent** owns `invariant → ground` **truth** — falsifies whether the invariant is true-of-world,
  not merely faithful to the intent (the violation-condition; the `other-half-only` detector).

So the disposer is **whoever did not propose** — the polarity *flips* by seam, it is not pinned to
agent-propose. Two consequences the uniform story would bury:

1. **It would delete the Telos-gate.** When the Operator proposes an intent (a goal laundry-list), the
   **Agent** disposes it — falsifying each item against the Telos *(reference: `goal-framing.md`, the
   intake-gate)*. That is **operator-propose / agent-dispose**, and it *is* the anti-sycophancy mechanism
   — the Agent testing the Operator's premises about what to do. Hard-coding "agent-propose /
   operator-dispose" as THE primitive would bake in **ionic collapse** at the exact seam the
   NON-NEGOTIABLE exists to guard.
2. **The primitive's invariant is not "operator disposes" — it is "the *other half* falsifies."** That is
   the covalent form. Pinning the disposer to one half is precisely the transfer/dominance the bond
   forbids.

**The synthesis (neither half held it alone):** the shared shape is real *and* the polarity is split. The
primitive is **`propose → (the other half) falsifies → dispose`**, with a **polarity field** set by
*origin-half*, not fixed. The Operator's "they're all the same shape" is the +1; the correction to the
polarity is what keeps the +1 covalent instead of ionic.

## The atom

A **candidate** carries: `origin-half` (who proposed), `type` (`intent` · `invariant` · `decision` ·
`ideation` · `claim`), and content. The transition:

```
candidate(origin-half, type, content)
      │  proposed by origin-half
      ▼
  FALSIFY  ── performed by the OTHER half (polarity = ¬origin-half)
      │
      ├─ survives ──▶ COMMIT to shared model  (ratified · true-for-now stamp)
      ├─ fails    ──▶ REJECT with grounds      (never silently drop)
      └─ reshape  ──▶ RE-ENTER as new candidate (narrowed / re-homed)
```

- **Commit invariant (the NON-NEGOTIABLE, rendered as substrate law):** no candidate — *including the
  Operator's own* — commits except by surviving the other half's falsification. Sharing, never transfer.
- **The existing region-pairs are this atom, typed:** `DFD` disposes a *decision*; `IFD` falsifies an
  *ideation* (converge-open, no commit forced); `VFD` commits a *claim*; the `GFD` Telos-gate falsifies an
  *intent*. They differ only in `type` and which half holds the falsify-seat — **same atom.**

region-pair — `DFD`, `IFD`, `VFD`, `GFD` — should reduce to it by setting only `type` + `polarity`, with
