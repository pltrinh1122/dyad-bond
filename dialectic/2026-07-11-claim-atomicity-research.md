## The question (Operator-raised, README claim-walkthrough arc)

Falsification should target a claim's **content**, not its **form** — and Claim 1's falsifier leaned on
the definition of "covalent" (Claim 4). Should C1 and C4 be **merged**, so attackers falsify only
content and can't quibble the definition?

## Method

Deep-research harness (Operator-directed, 2026-07-10/11): 5 parallel search angles (Popper's
conventionalist stratagems · Duhem–Quine · Lakatos hard-core/protective-belt · claim-atomicity norms ·
preregistration/operationalization practice), 104 agents, sources fetched and every extracted claim
adversarially verified by 3 independent refutation-voters (≥2/3 refutes kills). 22 claims survived;
3 stronger variants were killed in verification (recorded honestly below). Primary sources prioritized
(Popper, Lakatos, Quine; SEP/IEP secondary).

## Verdict — do NOT merge; keep the definition a separate, frozen, attackable claim

Every surviving line converged (survived 3-0 unless noted):

1. **Duhem–Quine (blame-localization)** — a refutation hits the *conjunction* (claim + definitions +
   auxiliaries); logic alone cannot assign the hit. Only explicit individuation of components lets
   judgment localize it. A merged C1+C4 makes any refutation **unassignable** (necessity broken, or
   definition broken?). *Dietrich & Honenberger, Synthese 197:337–354 (2020).*
2. **Popper (permanent criticizability)** — background knowledge, definitions included, is "open to
   criticism," never settled; folding the definition inside another claim *insulates* it — the
   anti-Popperian move. A separate C4 institutionalizes the criticizability. *Rubin, Synthese 206:111
   (2025), tracing to Conjectures & Refutations.*
3. **Definition theory (own falsifier-space)** — definitions carry their own graded
   refutation-conditions (extensional/intensional/sense adequacy) distinct in kind from the empirical
   claim's; and the **Conservativeness criterion** — a stipulation may not yield substantive new facts —
   is the textbook-formal analogue of C4's "smuggles the outcome back in" falsifier. *(SEP
   "Definitions"; 2-1 + 3-0 on the merged pair — the one non-unanimous survivor.)*
4. **Operationalization (a definition alone flips verdicts)** — re-analyzing fixed trial data varying
   *only one outcome definition* flipped the conclusion (OR 0.94, p=0.89 → OR 0.23, p<0.001); best
   practice pre-specifies definitions as distinct, decomposed, checkable gates. *Kahan & Jairath,
   Trials 2018.*
5. **Atomicity (measured, not taste)** — non-atomic hypothesis statements are literally uncountable by
   trained coders (14% concordance); structured formats measurably restrict degrees of freedom.
   *Bakker et al., PLOS Biology 2020.*
6. **Anti-HARKing** — post-hoc redefinition destroys a test's power to refute; the countermeasure is a
   frozen, explicit definitional commitment. A separate frozen C4 *is* the anti-no-true-Scotsman
   device. *Nosek et al. / preregistration literature (verifier confidence: medium).*
7. **Honest caveat (3 stronger claims killed in verification)** — the payoff is not the ceremony of
   pre-stating but *which* flexibility-reducing commitments are frozen; and prespecification's value is
   philosophy-dependent (Rubin vs. Mayo/Lakens is a live dispute). The frozen definition must itself
   stay criticizable — which is exactly C4-as-separate-claim.

**The reframe that answers the Operator's worry:** the definition *is* content (finding 3), and the
anti-quibbling protection sought from merging is achieved by **cross-referencing** instead — C1 is
judged against C4's definition *as frozen*; the definition moves only by its own explicit falsification.
