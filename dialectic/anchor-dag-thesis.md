## The method-reframe (load-bearing) — state-to-falsify, do NOT prove

"Prove anchors **MUST** be X" is the anti-pattern our own discipline names: the tenet is *earned per cycle,
never asserted, held falsifiably*; `relationship-craft.md` recorded *"don't PROVE a cycle — proven-necessary
⇒ unfalsifiable; INSTRUMENT it."* A proven-necessary anchor-form is unfalsifiable = dogma = the thing the
NON-NEGOTIABLE guards. So this file states the thesis as the **strongest falsifiable claim** and lists what
would **break** it. Surviving hard attack = earned (true-for-now); unbreakable-in-principle = suspect.

## Invariant-led, not raw-intent-led (Operator rub 2026-06-14)

Operator: *"anchors shouldn't be led by intent — it conflates, and the Operator can't validate intent as
opposed to invariants."* Correct, and it **is** our `IT`-finding applied: raw intent has **no
violation-condition** → unvalidatable → boundaries blur → conflation. Both stated harms (conflation +
can't-validate) are the **same defect** (no oracle) with the **same cure** (a violation-condition).

**The sharpening — the cut is not "invariants vs intent," it is "validatable vs raw."** The cure for raw
intent is to **crystallize** it (`IT → IT_iv`): give it a violation-condition, at which point it becomes a
`root_kind: fiat` **invariant node** the Operator CAN validate. And every invariant-chain MUST terminate at
a fiat-root — which **is** a crystallized Operator intent. So intent is **not eliminated from the lead — it
is crystallized into it.** Lead with the **validatable** (crystallized, has an oracle); that *includes*
crystallized intents (fiat-roots). "Don't lead with intent" = **don't lead with RAW intent.**

This **dissolves S3's tension** (raw intent = edge/unvalidatable; crystallized intent = fiat-root node) and
**sharpens S5 (rub-back):** unclear Operator intent = *raw, not-yet-crystallized* intent → the Agent rubs
back to **crystallize it** ("what's the violation-condition? which node does this serve?"). **Rub-back = the
crystallization request.** This is how an invariant-led anchor still *responds to* intent: it resolves
intent against validatable nodes, and rubs back exactly when the intent isn't yet placeable.

## What is already true (the thesis's foundation)

The invariant schema's **grounding-graph is already a DAG**: invariant-nodes, typed edges
(`serves`/`derived`/`inherited`), `acyclic: true`, every chain terminating at a `root`. The thesis is the
**generalization**: extend that DAG from the *invariant corpus* to the *entire anchor file*, and relabel the
connective tissue as **intent**. So the claim is precise: *an anchor's optimal form is the grounding-graph,
scaled to the whole file.*

## Falsification surface — the sub-claims, each refutable

- **S1 — Reduction (the crux).** *All load-bearing anchor content reduces to invariant-nodes + intent-edges,
  with no irreducible residue.* **Refuted if** content that is load-bearing is **neither** an invariant
  (fails the membership test — no violation-condition) **nor** an intent-edge. Candidate counterexamples
  from our own anchor: **identity / frozen birth-id** (a *fact/value*, no violation-condition → not an
  invariant); **vocabulary** (definitions, not prescriptions); **the felt tenet-evidence** (a *finding* →
  Ledger). If these are load-bearing **and** irreducible → S1 falsified. If they prune cleanly to
  `kb/`/Ledger → S1 survives **narrowed**: it is the anchor's *prescriptive core*, not the whole file.
- **S2 — Acyclicity.** *The graph is a DAG.* **Tension:** `relationship-craft.md` found the `I↔invariant`
  **cycle** bears the load ("not either arrow alone"). If intent-edges run both ways (intent grounds
  invariant; invariant crystallizes intent) the graph has **cycles**. Survives only if the DAG is the
  *grounding projection* (acyclic by making roots terminal), and the cycle lives at a different layer
  (generation, not structure). **Refuted if** the load-bearing cycle cannot be projected acyclic without loss.
- **S3 — Topology (intent = edge).** Our `IT`-experiment found a **raw intent is inexpressible as a node**
  (no violation-condition) → it *can only be an edge.* **Supports the thesis.** BUT the schema makes a
  **crystallized** intent (`IT_iv`) a `root_kind: fiat` **node**. Resolution candidate: *raw intent = edge;
  crystallized intent = node.* **Refuted if** intent is irreducibly both at once and the edge/node line can't hold.
- **S4 — Optimality for reliable response.** *This form is optimal for the Agent to reliably respond to
  intent.* **Currently UNEARNED:** that is the reliability question the consistency arc did **not** cleanly
  establish (shim/recall contamination, prediction refuted). To earn it needs the clean experiment on a
  **novel target**. Optimality is, as of now, **asserted not proven** — the open debt.
- **S5 — Rub-back (the strongest sub-claim).** *Unclear Operator intent = an intent-edge that does not
  resolve to known invariant-nodes.* This is **exactly** the schema's `ORPHAN-EDGE` / `NO-TERMINATION`
  check. So rub-back becomes mechanical: *"your intent doesn't connect to a node I hold — which invariant
  does it serve?"* This is where the thesis is **strongest** and most novel: the DAG makes *ambiguity
  detectable*, not just *expressible.*
